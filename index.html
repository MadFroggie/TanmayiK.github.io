Who I Am

About

"I approach data the way I approach books — I don't just scan for the answer. I want to understand the argument."

I'm a senior data scientist with a dual background in Computer Science and Finance. That combination isn't incidental — it's how I stay useful when the problem crosses the boundary between engineering and the business it's meant to serve.

My work spans the full ML lifecycle: feature engineering, model selection, deployment via Airflow-orchestrated pipelines, experiment tracking in MLflow, and long-running production monitoring with PSI-based drift detection. I've built systems that run for months without degradation — because I designed the monitoring first.

CFA Level 1 cleared. MBA in Finance. I've presented model outputs to actuaries, risk committees, and C-suite. I can write a SHAP waterfall plot explanation that a CFO will understand and a model card that a regulator won't redline.

Outside work: I read obsessively. Mostly non-fiction — Kahneman, Nate Silver, Hofstadter, Epstein, Rosling. I think the best data scientists share something with good readers: comfort with uncertainty, willingness to update on evidence, and a suspicion of their own priors.
